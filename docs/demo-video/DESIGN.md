# Amiweave Demo Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

A quiet, warm product film that feels like entering the existing Amiweave desktop app at night. Real interface captures remain the primary visual evidence. Typography and motion should support intimacy and trust: restrained editorial headings, precise product labels, generous negative space, slow focus pulls, and small coral accents. The result should feel human and technically credible rather than promotional or futuristic.

## Colors

- `#0C101C` - primary night canvas
- `#171522` - warm surface and transition field
- `#F6EEF2` - primary text
- `#B9AFB8` - secondary text
- `#EF8FA4` - coral accent drawn from the application

## Typography

- Display and Chinese statements: `Noto Serif SC`, `Songti SC`, serif
- Product labels and metadata: `IBM Plex Sans`, `Microsoft YaHei`, sans-serif

Use strong weight contrast: 800-900 for short display phrases, 300-400 for supporting copy. Headlines are at least 72px and body text at least 24px.

## Motion

- Calm build, breathe, resolve rhythm.
- Use focus pulls and blur crossfades as the primary transition language.
- Entrances may combine short horizontal movement, scale, and opacity; do not repeat one entrance pattern across a scene.
- Interface captures move only with subtle pans or scale pushes so the product remains inspectable.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not invent interface screens or obscure the real captures with decorative overlays.
- Do not use neon blue, purple gradients, glossy 3D objects, or particle fields.
- Do not use rapid glitch cuts, elastic motion, or loud advertising copy.
- Do not place long paragraphs on screen.
- Do not use rounded card grids as a generic visual motif.
